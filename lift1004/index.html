1. What did you learn?

We are all responsible for Reconciliation and are all on our own path. I was reminded of many things I already knew: treaties, the pass system, residential schools, the Truth and Reconciliation Report, etc. I was reminded of all the terrible things that have been done to Indiginous people.

Something new I learned was the significance behind the Wampum Belt. I am greatly moved by the amount of work put into and deep meaning behind the belts.

2. What do you still need to learn?

I know that I have a long way to go in my own path to Reconciliation. I need to do more and strive to integrate Indigenous ways of learning into my curriculum.

Adding authentic indiginous content into assessments is still something I struggle with. I am on a missing to find similar programs and other instructors that have been successful in integrating meaningful indigenous content into their curriculum.

3. How will you apply your learning?

Our program is working on integrating indigenous perspectives into our courses. Finding Elders, relevant professionals, and working to make partnerships with indigenous groups will get us on a better path.

I will continue to work on my land acknowledgements, striving to have practical actions for students to take and not just pre-scripted words.
