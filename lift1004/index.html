1. What did you learn?

We are all responsible for Reconciliation and are each on our own path. I was reminded of many things I had become familiar with through past learning: the establishment of treaties, the pass system, residential schools, the Truth and Reconciliation Report, etc. I was reminded of all of the ways in which colonization caused harm to Indigenous people.

Something new I learned was the significance behind the Wampum Belt. I am greatly moved by the amount of work put into and deep meaning behind the belts.

2. What do you still need to learn?

I know that I have a long way to go in my own path to Reconciliation. I will strive to integrate Indigenous ways of learning into my curriculum.

Incorporating authentic Indigenous content into assessments is still something I struggle with. I am on a mission to find similar programs and other instructors that have been successful in integrating meaningful Indigenous content into their curriculum and assessments.

3. How will you apply your learning?

Our program is working on integrating Indigenous perspectives into our courses. Establishing connections with Elders and relevant professionals, and working to make partnerships with Indigenous groups will get us on a better path.

I will continue to share regular land acknowledgements in my courses, striving to incorporate my own commitments along with practical actions for students to take.
