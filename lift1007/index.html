Microsoft Forms

Saskatchewan Polytechnic is a "microsoft shop" at its core. Students and Faculty are provided with educational Microsoft 365 accounts and many other applications require single sign-on using these accounts. Yes many people choose to use alternatives like Google Docs, Survey Monkey, etc. which reduce security and spread the internal technology ecosystem thin. Why not just learn to use the software we already have access to!?

Description

Microsoft Forms allows users to create surveys and quizzes. They can be entirely anonymous, or record user information. There are many competing software solutions, but Google Forms is the most notable.

Support

  • All Sask Polytech students and faculty have Microsoft 365 accounts.
  • Single sign-on (SSO) works with campus credentials.
  • No extra app required - runs in a browser and mobile apps exist if wanted.

Features

  • Question types: choice, text, rating, Likert, date, file upload, etc.
  • Quizzes with points, answer keys, and automatic feedback.
  • Branching logic to show different questions based on answers.
  • Real-time charts; export to Excel for deeper analysis.
  • Share options: only people in the org, specific people, or anyone with the link.

Benefits

  • Fast to build; easy for students to answer on phone or laptop.
  • Uses accounts students already have (reduces tool sprawl).
  • Simple analytics; quick pulse checks or graded quizzes.

Limitations

  • Less design control than dedicated survey tools.
  • Advanced branching and reporting are basic compared to premium survey suites.

Classroom

  • Mid-term feedback survey (anonymous).
  • Quick entry/exit tickets to check understanding.
  • Low-stakes quizzes with auto-grading.
  • Idea voting / peer review forms.

Analysis

Microsoft Forms is safe, accessible, and equitable within Sask Polytech's Microsoft 365 system, making it a reliable choice for surveys, quizzes, and feedback.

UDL

  • Multiple means of engagement: quick check-ins lower pressure and increase participation.
  • Multiple means of representation: mix text, images, and short videos in questions.
  • Multiple means of action/expression: allow text answers, file uploads, or links to artefacts.
  • Accessibility: keyboard navigation, screen reader support, clear labels; keep questions concise.

Safety

  • Use org-only links to keep responses inside Sask Polytech.
  • Avoid collecting sensitive personal information unless necessary.
  • Limit who can view results; remove identifiers when sharing summaries.

Ethics

  • Tell students why you're collecting data and how it will be used.
  • Make anonymous options available for feedback when possible.
  • Collect minimum data needed; set clear retention timelines.

Equity

  • All students have Microsoft 365 accounts; no extra sign-ups.
  • Mobile-friendly; provide extra time or alternative formats if needed.
  • Offer plain-language questions; allow rereads and retries for low-stakes checks.
