Microsoft Forms

Saskatchewan Polytechnic is a "microsoft shop" at its core. Students and Faculty are provided with educational Microsoft 365 accounts and many other applications require single sign-on using these accounts. Yes many people choose to use alternatives like Google Docs, Survey Monkey, etc. which reduce security and spread the internal technology ecosystem thin. Why not just learn to use the software we already have access to!?

Description

Microsoft Forms allows users to create surveys and quizzes. They can be entirely anonymous, or record user information. There are many competing software solutions, but Google Forms is the most notable.

Support

  • All students and faculty have Microsoft 365 accounts.
  • Single sign-on (SSO) works with campus credentials.
  • No extra app required - runs in a browser and mobile apps.

Features

  • Different question types including choice, text, rating, likert, date, etc.
  • Quizzes with points, answer keys, and automatic feedback.
  • Real-time charts.
  • Share options to control visibility.

Benefits

  • Fast to build.
  • Uses accounts students already have.
  • Simple analytics.

Limitations

  • Less design control than dedicated survey tools.
  • Reporting is basic compared to premium survey suites.

Classroom

  • Mid-term feedback survey (anonymous).
  • Quick entry/exit tickets to check understanding.
  • Low-stakes quizzes with auto-grading.
  • Idea voting / peer review forms.

Analysis

Microsoft Forms is safe, accessible, and equitable within Saskatchewan Polytechnic's Microsoft 365 system, making it a reliable choice for surveys, quizzes, and feedback.

UDL

  • Engagement: quick check-ins lower pressure and increase participation.
  • Representation: mix text, images, and short videos in questions.
  • Accessibility: keyboard navigation, screen reader support, clear labels; keep questions concise.

Safety

  • Use org-only links to keep responses inside organization.
  • Avoid collecting sensitive personal information.
  • Limit who can view results; remove identifiers when sharing summaries.

Ethics

  • Tell students why you're collecting data.
  • Make anonymous options available for feedback.
  • Set clear data retention timelines.

Equity

  • All students have accounts; no extra sign-ups.
  • Provide extra time if needed.
  • Allow retries for low-stakes checks.
