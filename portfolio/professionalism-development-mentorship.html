Professionalism, Development, & Mentorship

The Professionalism, Development, and Mentorship competency focuses on enhancing instructional practice by engaging in lifelong learning and maintaining current knowledge, skills and practices from both the scholarship of teaching and learning and industry. In addition, this competency addresses the importance of supporting Saskatchewan Polytechnic policies, initiatives and values and incorporating leadership skills, behaviours and attitudes into your practice.

Emerging Leaders

I was fortunate to receive funding to participate in an Emerging Leaders program during the 2024/2025 school year. At the end of the program , I interviewed two industry leaders and presented to my cohort.

This was a truly amazing experience to be learning alongside other very engaged faculty. It really helped round out my leadership strategies and helped build a solid network of colleagues outside my school.

My final presentation to the cohort was an interview with two different leaders I look up to: Heather Adams (Co-Founder & CEO of Rock & Bloom) and Katherine Regnier (Founder & CEO of Coconut Software). Both of these leaders recommended trust as the underlying value of their companies and leadership styles.

Professional Development Plan

Saskatchewan Polytechnic is committed to supporting faculty professional development. Part of this commitment is for faculty to create a professional development plan. The goal of the professional development plan is for faculty to set professional and instructional goals based on Saskatchewan Polytechnic's seven instructional competencies, reflect on learning growth, and strive for continuous development.
