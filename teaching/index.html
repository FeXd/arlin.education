My Teaching Philosophy, Approach, Principles

This page will contain information and resources gathered during my teaching journey which align with my beliefs.

  • Accessibility: Ensure all course materials and activities are accessible to students with diverse needs.
  • Universal Design: Implement universal design principles to create inclusive and flexible learning environments.
  • Adaptability: Recognize that every cohort is unique and adapt teaching methods accordingly.
  • Diverse Learning Styles: Acknowledge that students learn in different ways and incorporate various instructional strategies to cater to diverse learning preferences.
  • Recorded Lectures: Record lectures to provide students with the opportunity to review content at their own pace.
  • Transparency: Maintain transparency in both lessons and evaluation criteria to build trust and clarity.
  • Student-Centered Learning: Focus on student-centered learning, promoting active engagement and participation.
  • Continuous Feedback: Provide regular and constructive feedback to support student growth and development.
  • Cultural Sensitivity: Be culturally sensitive and inclusive, respecting the diverse backgrounds of all students.
  • Lifelong Learning: Encourage a mindset of lifelong learning and curiosity in students.
  • Technology Integration: Leverage technology to enhance learning experiences and provide additional resources.
  • Collaborative Learning: Promote collaborative learning opportunities to foster teamwork and peer support.
  • Critical Thinking: Encourage critical thinking and problem-solving skills through challenging and thought-provoking activities.
  • Reflective Practice: Engage in reflective practice to continuously improve teaching methods and strategies.
  • Supportive Environment: Create a supportive and safe learning environment where students feel comfortable to express their ideas and take risks.
